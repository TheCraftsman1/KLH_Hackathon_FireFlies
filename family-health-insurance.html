What is Family Health Insurance?

A Family Floater Health Insurance plan covers your entire family — self, spouse, dependent children, and even parents — under a single sum insured. It's more affordable than buying separate individual policies for each member.

For example, a ₹10 Lakh family floater can be used by any family member for hospitalization. If one member uses ₹3 Lakh, the remaining ₹7 Lakh is available for others.

Key Benefits

  • Cost-Effective: 30-40% cheaper than individual policies for each member
  • Shared Sum Insured: Entire family covered under one pool
  • Maternity & Newborn: Delivery expenses and newborn cover from day 1
  • No Claim Bonus: Sum insured grows every claim-free year (up to 100%)
  • Free Health Checkup: Annual health checkup for all members
  • Tax Benefits: Deduction under Section 80D — up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens)

FAQs

Who can be covered under a family floater?
Self, spouse, up to 4 dependent children, and parents (including in-laws in some plans). Some insurers also allow siblings and grandparents.
What happens if the sum insured runs out?
Many plans offer "Restore" or "Recharge" benefits that automatically refill the sum insured if it gets exhausted. HDFC Optima Restore, for example, provides 100% restoration unlimited times.
Is it better than individual plans?
For young families (2+2), a floater is definitely more cost-effective. However, if parents are above 55, separate individual policies may sometimes be better to avoid age-loading.
