What is Workmen Compensation Insurance?

Workmen Compensation Insurance (also called Employees' Compensation Insurance) is a mandatory policy that protects employers against their legal liability to compensate employees for work-related injuries, occupational diseases, or death. Under the Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 (amended as Employees' Compensation Act), every employer is legally required to compensate employees for workplace injuries.

This policy transfers the employer's liability to the insurance company, protecting the business from potentially devastating compensation claims that could run into lakhs of rupees.

What's Covered

  • Death Compensation: Lump sum payment to deceased employee's dependents
  • Permanent Total Disability: Full compensation for total loss of earning capacity
  • Permanent Partial Disability: Proportionate compensation based on disability percentage
  • Temporary Disability: Half-monthly payments during recovery period
  • Occupational Diseases: Diseases contracted due to nature of employment
  • Medical Expenses: Treatment costs for work-related injuries

FAQs

Is workmen compensation insurance mandatory?
Yes, under the Employees' Compensation Act, 1923 (formerly Workmen's Compensation Act), all employers are legally liable to compensate employees for work-related injuries. While the insurance itself may not be legally compulsory, it's the only practical way to manage this statutory liability.
Which employees are covered?
The Act covers all employees earning up to ₹15,000/month, including workers in factories, mines, plantations, construction sites, and other establishments. However, businesses can (and should) extend coverage to all employees through the insurance policy.
How is the premium calculated?
Premium is calculated based on the nature of work (risk category), number of employees, total annual wages, and past claim history. Higher-risk industries like construction and mining have higher premiums compared to office-based businesses.
