What is Marine Insurance?

Marine Insurance protects goods, cargo, and vessels against losses or damage during transit by sea, air, road, or rail. It's essential for businesses involved in import/export, manufacturing, and distribution.

Marine insurance covers risks like shipwreck, piracy, theft, damage due to handling, natural perils, and general average contributions. It can be taken for single shipments or as an open/annual policy for regular consignments.

Key Benefits

  • Cargo Protection: Covers goods in transit against damage, theft, and loss
  • Multi-Modal Coverage: Sea, air, road, rail — all modes covered
  • Invoice Value + Profit: Cover up to 110% of invoice value including expected profit
  • Warehouse to Warehouse: Coverage from origin warehouse to destination warehouse
  • Natural Perils: Covers storm, flood, earthquake, lightning damage
  • Open Policy: Annual policies for businesses with regular shipments

FAQs

Is marine insurance only for sea transport?
No, despite the name, marine insurance covers goods in transit by any mode — sea, air, road, and rail. It protects cargo from origin to destination regardless of the transportation method used.
What is an open marine policy?
An open marine policy is an annual policy for businesses with regular shipments. Instead of buying insurance for each shipment, you declare consignments as they happen under one umbrella policy, making it more convenient and cost-effective.
Does marine insurance cover piracy?
Yes, marine insurance covers piracy under the "All Risk" policy. However, it's important to check the specific terms as some policies may have exclusions for certain high-risk zones.
