What is Travel Insurance?

Travel insurance protects you against unexpected events during your domestic or international trips. From medical emergencies abroad to trip cancellations, lost passports, and delayed flights — it covers the financial impact of travel mishaps.

Medical treatment abroad can cost lakhs — a simple hospital visit in the US can cost ₹5-10 Lakh. Travel insurance ensures you're never stranded without funds in a foreign country.

What's Covered

  • Medical Emergencies: Hospitalization, doctor visits, surgery abroad
  • Trip Cancellation: Reimbursement of non-refundable bookings
  • Lost Baggage: Compensation for lost, stolen, or delayed luggage
  • Flight Delay: Hotel & meal expenses for delays over 6-12 hours
  • Passport Loss: Expenses for emergency passport replacement
  • Emergency Evacuation: Air ambulance & medical evacuation costs
  • Personal Liability: If you accidentally damage third-party property
  • Adventure Sports: Optional cover for trekking, scuba, skiing

FAQs

Is travel insurance mandatory for Schengen visa?
Yes, travel insurance with minimum €30,000 (approx. $35,000) medical cover is mandatory for all Schengen visa applications. Most European countries require it.
When should I buy travel insurance?
Buy it as soon as you book your trip. This way, you're covered for pre-departure cancellations too. Most plans allow purchase up to the day before departure.
Does it cover COVID-19?
Yes, most current travel insurance plans cover COVID-19 related hospitalization, quarantine expenses, and trip cancellation due to positive test results.
