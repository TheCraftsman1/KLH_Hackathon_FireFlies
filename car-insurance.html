What is Car Insurance?

Car insurance is a financial safety net that covers damages to your vehicle and third-party liabilities arising from accidents, theft, fire, or natural disasters. As per the Motor Vehicles Act, at least third-party liability insurance is mandatory for every car on Indian roads.

A comprehensive car insurance policy protects you against own damage, third-party liability, personal accident cover, and even covers accessories and modifications.

What's Covered

  • Own Damage: Accidents, collisions, overturning, fire, explosion, theft
  • Third-Party Liability: Damage to other's vehicle or property, bodily injury
  • Personal Accident: ₹15 Lakh cover for owner-driver
  • Natural Disasters: Floods, earthquakes, storms, cyclones
  • Roadside Assistance: 24/7 towing, flat tire, battery jumpstart
  • Zero Depreciation: Full claim without depreciation deduction (add-on)
  • Engine Protect: Covers engine damage from waterlogging (add-on)

FAQs

Is car insurance mandatory in India?
Yes, at least third-party car insurance is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. Driving without insurance can result in a fine of ₹2,000 for the first offence and ₹4,000 for subsequent offences.
What is IDV (Insured Declared Value)?
IDV is the maximum amount you'll receive if your car is stolen or totally damaged. It's calculated as the manufacturer's selling price minus depreciation based on the car's age.
What is No Claim Bonus (NCB)?
NCB is a discount on your premium for every claim-free year. It starts at 20% after the first year and goes up to 50% after 5 consecutive claim-free years.
