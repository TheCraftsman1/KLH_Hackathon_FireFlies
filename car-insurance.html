What's Covered in Car Insurance?

Understand what your policy protects and what it doesn't

What's Covered

  • Accidents, collisions & overturning
  • Theft of your vehicle
  • Fire, explosion & self-ignition
  • Natural calamities (flood, earthquake, cyclone)
  • Third-party bodily injury & property damage
  • Personal accident cover for owner-driver (₹15L)
  • Riots, strikes & malicious damage
  • Transit damage (rail, road, air, water)
  • Terrorism damage

What's NOT Covered

  • Normal wear and tear / depreciation
  • Mechanical or electrical breakdown
  • Driving without a valid license
  • Driving under influence of alcohol/drugs
  • Intentional or deliberate damage
  • Using car for commercial purposes
  • Consequential losses
  • War or nuclear risks
  • Damage outside geographical area

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about car insurance

Is car insurance mandatory in India?
Yes, at least third-party car insurance is mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. Driving without insurance can result in a fine of ₹2,000 for the first offence and ₹4,000 for subsequent offences, along with possible imprisonment for up to 3 months.
What is IDV (Insured Declared Value)?
IDV is the current market value of your car and represents the maximum amount you'll receive if it's stolen or totally damaged. It's calculated as: Manufacturer's Selling Price - Depreciation (based on age). A higher IDV means higher premium but better payout, while a lower IDV reduces premium but also reduces the claim amount.
What is No Claim Bonus (NCB)?
NCB is a reward in the form of a discount on your OD premium for every claim-free year. It starts at 20% after the first year and goes up to 50% after 5 consecutive claim-free years: Year 1: 20% → Year 2: 25% → Year 3: 35% → Year 4: 45% → Year 5+: 50%. NCB is transferable to a new car and even a new insurer.
What's the difference between third-party and comprehensive?
Third-party (TP) insurance only covers damage you cause to others — it's the minimum legal requirement. Comprehensive insurance covers both third-party liability AND damage to your own vehicle from accidents, theft, fire, natural disasters, etc. We strongly recommend comprehensive cover for complete protection.
How do I file a car insurance claim?
1) Inform the insurer immediately via helpline or app. 2) File an FIR if required (theft, third-party damage). 3) Get a surveyor inspection. 4) Take your car to a network garage for cashless repair, or get repairs done and submit bills for reimbursement. Most claims are settled within 7 working days.
Can I transfer my car insurance to a new owner?
Yes, car insurance can be transferred to a new owner within 14 days of the ownership transfer. Both buyer and seller must submit a joint application to the insurer along with the sale deed, new RC copy, and identity documents. The NCB, however, stays with the original policyholder.
What add-ons should I buy?
The top recommended add-ons are: Zero Depreciation (must-have for cars under 5 years), Engine Protect (for flood-prone areas), Roadside Assistance (for frequent travelers), and Return to Invoice (for new or expensive cars). These add-ons cost relatively little but provide significant additional protection.
How can I reduce my car insurance premium?
You can lower your premium by: 1) Maintaining NCB (up to 50% discount), 2) Opting for a higher voluntary deductible, 3) Installing ARAI-approved anti-theft devices (2.5% discount), 4) Choosing the right IDV, 5) Buying online (saves 5-15%), and 6) Comparing quotes from multiple insurers through Insurix.India.
