What is Professional Indemnity Insurance?

Professional Indemnity (PI) Insurance for doctors protects medical practitioners against claims arising from medical negligence, malpractice, or errors during treatment. With rising malpractice lawsuits and consumer court cases, PI insurance is now essential for every doctor in India.

It covers legal defense costs, compensation awarded by courts, and settlement amounts — ensuring your personal assets and savings are protected from patient lawsuits.

What's Covered

  • Medical Negligence Claims: Defense against malpractice allegations
  • Legal Defense Costs: Lawyer fees, court costs, and expert witness fees
  • Compensation Awards: Damages ordered by courts or consumer forums
  • Good Samaritan Cover: Protection for emergency treatment provided
  • Locum Cover: Coverage when a substitute doctor fills in
  • Disciplinary Proceedings: Defense before Medical Council of India

FAQs

Is PI insurance mandatory for doctors in India?
While not legally mandatory for all doctors, the Indian Medical Association strongly recommends it. Some hospitals require their empaneled doctors to have PI insurance. Given the rise in consumer forum cases, it's practically essential.
Does PI insurance cover past incidents?
Most PI policies are on a "claims-made" basis, meaning they cover claims made during the policy period, even if the incident occurred before (subject to a retroactive date). Some policies also offer "run-off" cover for claims after retirement.
Are cosmetic procedures covered?
Yes, but premiums may be higher for cosmetic/plastic surgery due to higher litigation risk. It's important to declare all procedures you perform when applying for PI insurance to ensure full coverage.
