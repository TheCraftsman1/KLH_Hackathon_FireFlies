What is Commercial Vehicle Insurance?

Commercial Vehicle Insurance covers vehicles used for business purposes — trucks, buses, taxis, auto-rickshaws, tankers, and fleet vehicles. Third-party liability insurance is mandatory by law under the Motor Vehicles Act for all commercial vehicles.

A comprehensive policy additionally covers own damage from accidents, theft, fire, natural calamities, and third-party liability, providing complete protection for your business on wheels.

Coverage Includes

  • Third-Party Liability: Mandatory cover for damage to third-party person or property
  • Own Damage: Covers damage to your vehicle from accidents, fire, theft
  • Driver/Cleaner Cover: Personal accident cover for driver and helpers
  • Goods in Transit: Optional cover for goods being transported
  • Natural Calamities: Flood, earthquake, storm, and hailstorm damage
  • Roadside Assistance: 24/7 breakdown assistance across India

FAQs

Is commercial vehicle insurance mandatory?
Yes, third-party liability insurance is mandatory by law under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 for all commercial vehicles plying on Indian roads. Driving without insurance can result in fines up to ₹2,000 and/or imprisonment up to 3 months.
What is IDV in commercial vehicle insurance?
IDV (Insured Declared Value) is the maximum sum insured for your vehicle, calculated as the manufacturer's listed selling price minus depreciation. It represents the approximate current market value of your vehicle.
Can I insure my entire fleet under one policy?
Yes, fleet insurance policies allow you to cover all your commercial vehicles under a single policy, often at a discounted premium. This simplifies management and renewal of multiple vehicle insurances.
