What is Group Health Insurance?

Group Health Insurance (GHI) is a corporate health insurance policy purchased by employers to cover their employees and optionally their families. It's one of the most valued employee benefits — 73% of employees consider health insurance a top factor in job satisfaction.

GHI offers significant advantages over individual plans: no medical tests, no waiting period for pre-existing conditions, lower premiums, and maternity cover from day 1.

FAQs

What is the minimum group size?
Most insurers require a minimum of 7-20 employees. Some insurers offer micro-group plans for as few as 7 employees (including startups and small businesses).
Are dependents covered?
Yes, employers can choose to cover employees + spouse, employees + family (spouse + 2 children), or employees + parents at additional premiums.
What happens when an employee leaves?
The employee can port (transfer) their group policy to an individual health insurance plan without losing continuity benefits. This must be done within 30 days of leaving the job.
