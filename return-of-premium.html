What is Return of Premium (TROP)?

A Term Return of Premium (TROP) plan works exactly like a regular term insurance — if the policyholder dies during the term, the nominee gets the full sum assured. But here's the bonus: if you survive the policy term, you get 100% of all premiums paid back!

It's perfect for people who don't want to "lose" their premium money. Though premiums are higher than regular term plans (~40-50% more), you get everything back if nothing happens.

How It Works

  • If you die during the term: Nominee gets full Sum Assured (e.g., ₹1 Crore)
  • If you survive the term: You get 100% of all premiums paid back
  • Tax-Free Return: Maturity amount is tax-free under Section 10(10D)
  • Critical Illness Rider: Optional add-on for 34+ critical illnesses
  • Accidental Death: Additional sum assured for accidental death
  • Zero Net Cost: Effectively free insurance if you survive

FAQs

Is TROP better than regular term insurance?
It depends on your preference. Regular term insurance has lower premiums, and if you invest the difference in mutual funds, you may get better returns. But if you prefer guaranteed money-back and don't want to manage separate investments, TROP is excellent.
Is the returned premium taxable?
No, the maturity amount from a TROP plan is tax-free under Section 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act, provided the premium doesn't exceed 10% of the sum assured.
Can I surrender the policy early?
Yes, but you'll only receive the surrender value (which is lower than total premiums paid). It's best to continue till maturity to get 100% premium return.
