What is D&O Liability Insurance?

Directors & Officers (D&O) Liability Insurance protects the personal assets of corporate directors, officers, and senior managers when they are sued for wrongful acts committed in their managerial capacity. Under Indian law, directors can be held personally liable for company decisions.

D&O insurance covers legal defense costs, settlements, and judgments arising from claims by shareholders, employees, regulators, customers, or competitors against the company's leadership.

Key Benefits

  • Personal Asset Protection: Protects directors' homes, savings, and investments
  • Legal Defense Costs: Covers lawyer fees, court expenses, and expert fees
  • Regulatory Proceedings: Defense before SEBI, RBI, MCA, and other regulators
  • Shareholder Lawsuits: Protection from investor/shareholder litigation
  • Employment Practices: Claims from wrongful termination, harassment allegations
  • Entity Coverage: Can extend to cover the company itself

FAQs

Is D&O insurance mandatory in India?
While not legally mandatory for all companies, SEBI regulations effectively require it for listed companies. For private companies and startups, it's strongly recommended as directors face increasing personal liability risks under the Companies Act, 2013.
Does D&O insurance cover fraud?
D&O insurance typically covers defense costs for fraud allegations until a final judgment. However, if fraud is proven, the policy generally excludes coverage for the actual damages. The key benefit is that defense costs are still covered during the legal process.
Who should buy D&O insurance?
Any company with a board of directors should consider D&O insurance. It's especially critical for listed companies, startups with investors, companies in regulated sectors, and organizations going through M&A, IPO, or significant growth phases.
