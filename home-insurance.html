What is Home Insurance?

Home Insurance protects your house structure and its contents against unforeseen events such as fire, floods, earthquakes, storms, burglary, and more. It typically comes in two parts — Structure Insurance (the building itself) and Contents Insurance (furniture, electronics, valuables inside).

In India, less than 1% of homeowners have home insurance, making it one of the most under-insured asset categories despite housing being the average family's largest investment.

What's Covered?

  • Fire & Explosion: Damage from fire, lightning, explosion, or implosion
  • Natural Disasters: Earthquake, flood, storm, cyclone, landslide, hurricane
  • Burglary & Theft: Loss of household contents due to break-in
  • Water Damage: Burst pipes, overflowing tanks, accidental water leakage
  • Riot & Strikes: Damage from civil disturbances and malicious acts
  • Alternative Accommodation: Temporary living expenses if home is uninhabitable
  • Liability Cover: Third-party injury or property damage on your premises

FAQs

Does home insurance cover tenant belongings?
Yes! As a tenant, you can buy a Contents-only policy to cover your furniture, electronics, clothes, and valuables. You don't need to insure the building structure — that's the landlord's responsibility.
Is earthquake covered?
Earthquake cover is usually available as an add-on or included in comprehensive plans. Basic fire policies may not include it. Always check your policy documents and opt for earthquake cover if you live in seismic zones.
How is the sum insured decided?
For structure, it's based on the reconstruction cost of your home (not market value). For contents, you should list all major items and their replacement values. Under-insuring can lead to proportional claim settlement.
What is NOT covered?
Common exclusions include: normal wear and tear, war/nuclear risks, damage from pets, intentional damage, damage to items outside the home (like garden), and items not declared in the policy.
